2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class Overview

Is the 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class a Good Used Car?

We named the Mercedes-Benz E-Class our 2013 Best Luxury Full-Size Car for Families. It remains a standout vehicle in a class of exceptional automobiles. The E-Class comes in four body styles – sedan, coupe, convertible, and wagon – and with powertrains that range from fuel efficient to extremely potent. Some rivals have more engaging handling. Still, the E-Class offers a smooth, refined ride.  

Used 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class Performance and Interior

The rear-wheel-drive 2013 Mercedes-Benz E350 has a 302-horsepower V6 engine that puts down more than enough power for everyday commutes. A seven-speed automatic transmission comes standard. The E350 BlueTec comes with a turbocharged diesel V6 engine that uses less fuel than the base model’s V6. The E400 Hybrid’s V6 engine and electric motor provide a balance of power and fuel efficiency. The all-wheel-drive E550 4Matic and E63 AMG models offer the quickest acceleration in the lineup with their potent twin-turbocharged V8 engines. 

Some other luxury cars have crisper handling, but the E-Class balances composure and comfort with plenty of stopping power. The E63 AMG features a sport suspension and an Eco stop-start system to conserve fuel. With the base engine, the E350 achieves 20 mpg in the city and 29 mpg on the highway, which is above average for the class. The E400 Hybrid achieves 24/30 mpg city/highway. 

 Read more about E-Class performance »

The 2013 Mercedes E-Class features a high-end interior with handsome finishes. The front seats offer ample power adjustments and remain comfortable on lengthy trips. The back seats have adult-friendly legroom. Sedan and wagon models have two full sets of LATCH car-seat connectors in the rear outboard seats and a tether-only connector in the rear middle seat. The E350 sedan has a good amount of trunk space, at 15.9 cubic feet.

The 2013 E-Class comes with Mercedes’ COMAND infotainment system with a 7-inch display. The system’s console-mounted knob and menu structure may seem complicated at first, but it gets easier to use with time. Available features include navigation, a panoramic moonroof, an iPod interface, satellite radio, and a rear-seat entertainment system. As with any older car, your portable devices may or may not sync up will with the 2013 E-Class’ technology.

Read more about E-Class interior »

Used 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class Prices

The average price of a used 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class sedan ranges from about $20,200 for the Luxury trim with rear-wheel drive, up to nearly $42,800 for the E 63 AMG model with 4Matic all-wheel drive, a V8 engine, and a sport-tuned suspension. However, pricing varies depending on the car’s mileage, condition, location, and features.

How Safe Is the E-Class?

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class has a perfect safety score for the class, matching several of its rivals. The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety named it a Top Safety Pick and gave it the highest rating of Good in all four crash evaluations. It earned a perfect five stars in the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration rollover crash test.  

The 2013 E-Class is available with several active safety features, including a rearview camera, parking sensors, adaptive cruise control, night vision with pedestrian detection, lane departure warning, and blind spot monitoring. 

See E-Class safety scores »

Is the 2013 E-Class the Best Model Year to Buy?

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class is part of a generation that ended with the 2016 model year. The 2011 and 2012 model years are nearly identical to the 2013 E-Class, though they lack standard heated front seats in the coupe and cabriolet models. Selecting a 2011 or 2012 E-Class would probably cost you less. 

The E-Class Hybrid is only available for 2013 and later model years.  

Compare the 2011, 2012, and 2013 E-Class » 

Which Used Mercedes-Benz E-Class Is Right for Me?

The 2013 Mercedes E-Class – available as a sedan, coupe, convertible, or wagon – has three trims: E350 (base), E350 BlueTec, and E550. The Mercedes E400 Hybrid and the high-performance E63 AMG are also available. 

Most luxury car shoppers will want to skip the base E350 model, as its only standard features are a sunroof, Bluetooth, and the COMAND infotainment system. Higher trims are defined by their powertrain, and standard features remain the same throughout the lineup. Most trims and body styles are available with packages that add technology and comfort features. For example, the Premium 1 package adds navigation, a Harman Kardon surround-sound stereo, satellite radio, heated front seats, and a rearview camera.

Mercedes-Benz certified pre-owned vehicles come with the balance of the original four-year/50,000-mile new-car warranty, plus an additional one-year/unlimited-mileage warranty. If the new-car warranty is expired, you’ll just receive the one-year/unlimited-mileage warranty. Every Mercedes-Benz CPO vehicle must pass a 164-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Mercedes-Benz warranty page carefully. The car’s original date of purchase determines if the model you’re considering qualifies as a CPO model.

2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class or 2013 Lexus GS?

While the E-Class has more engine options, the Lexus GS is a better fit if you want ride comfort without sacrificing engaging driving dynamics. The GS also has a perfect reliability score, but like the E-Class, it has a complicated infotainment system.

Which Is Better: 2013 Mercedes-Benz E-Class or 2013 Audi A6?

The Audi A6 boasts a balanced ride and one of the most luxurious, well-made cabins in the class. It also comes with more standard features than the E-Class, including an iPod interface, satellite radio, and leather upholstery. However, the A6 has a lower reliability rating than the E-Class. Also, Audi’s MMI infotainment system is one of the least user-friendly systems in the class. The E-Class is the better choice.
