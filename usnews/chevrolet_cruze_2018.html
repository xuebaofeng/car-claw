2018 Chevrolet Cruze Review

by Sarah Shelton | July 19, 2018

The 2018 Chevrolet Cruze is an excellent compact car for commuters, on account of its roomy seats, superb fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride. Its low starting price and long list of standard features make it a great value and a strong contender in the class.

Is the Chevrolet Cruze a Good Car?

The Chevy Cruze is a good compact car. The Cruze provides a smooth and comfortable ride, and it offers some of the best fuel economy ratings in its class. It also rates well for crash safety and predicted reliability. It does have its drawbacks, though. The Cruze offers pokey acceleration, and its fuel-saving engine stop-start system – which can’t be deactivated – feels unrefined.

Should I Buy the Chevrolet Cruze?

With its exceptional fuel economy rating (especially with the diesel-powered model), the Chevy Cruze is a good choice if you do a lot of highway driving. It has supportive seats and a quiet ride. Android Auto and Apple CarPlay come standard, putting many of your favorite smartphone apps at your fingertips.

Still, this is an exceptionally competitive class. There are roomier alternatives, such as the Toyota Corolla or Kia Soul. There are also sportier alternatives. The Honda Civic offers brisker acceleration with its available engines, and the Mazda3 has more athletic handling.

Should I Buy a New or Used Chevrolet Cruze?

Chevrolet fully redesigned the Cruze for the 2016 model year, and the Cruze has seen a couple notable changes since then. Originally only available as a sedan with a 153-horsepower, turbocharged engine, the Cruze added hatchback and diesel versions for 2017. The hatchback has more cargo space than the sedan, while the 137-horsepower diesel gets great fuel economy (30 mpg city/52 mpg highway). Additionally, Chevy's Teen Driver system became standard for 2017.

You can likely save money by shopping for a used Cruze instead of a new one. If you want a diesel-powered hatchback Cruze, you'll have to shop for a 2018 model. For 2017, these models were mutually exclusive. To further research used models in this generation, read our reviews of the 2016 and 2017 Chevrolet Cruze. If you decide an older model is right for you, check out our Used Car Deals page to learn about savings and discounts on used vehicles.

How Much Does the Chevrolet Cruze Cost?

The Chevy Cruze is one of the least expensive compact cars you can buy. Its starting price of $16,975 gets you the sedan body style, a turbocharged gasoline engine, and a manual transmission. Prices start at $21,320 for the hatchback version. Both body styles are available with a turbodiesel engine, with prices ranging from $23,820 to $25,520. Chevrolet Cruze Versus the Competition

Which Is Better: Chevrolet Cruze or Honda Civic?

It’s hard to compete against the Honda Civic, which is one of the most popular and well-rounded compact cars on the market. Its fuel economy is comparable to the Cruze’s, but its handling is more agile. Both the Civic and the Cruze have a high-quality interior. However, the Civic has plenty of space for five, one of the largest trunks in the class, and excellent safety scores. Both are good cars, but the Civic is better.

Which Is Better: Chevrolet Cruze or Hyundai Elantra?

If you look at the Chevrolet Cruze and Hyundai Elantra side by side, they are similar in a lot of ways, but the Cruze has a slight advantage. For example, the Cruze's standard features list is much longer. You'll find amenities that cost extra in the Elantra, such as Apple CarPlay and a touch-screen display screen.

Which Is Better: Chevrolet Cruze or Ford Focus?

For the most part, the Ford Focus' acceleration falls in line with that of the Cruze. There are a couple high-performance Focus models (the ST and RS hatchbacks) that the Cruze can't compete with, though they're a little pricey. Nonetheless, the Cruze has a roomier rear seat and a better assortment of standard technology, but it comes at a slightly higher price point. Both are fine options, but most shoppers will prefer the Cruze.

Cruze Interior

How Many People Does the Cruze Seat?

In many small cars, it's a squeeze when you fill every seat. The 2018 Cruze, on the other hand, can comfortably carry five people. A substantial amount of shoulder room and leg space is available in both the front and back seats, and three adults can share the back for short trips. The seats are well-cushioned and properly bolstered and come with standard cloth upholstery or upgraded leather.

Cruze and Car Seats

There's enough room in the back for two child safety seats. LATCH hardware includes three tether anchors and two lower anchors in the back seats. The tether anchors are conveniently located and easy to use. The lower anchors, however, are a little more challenging. They're tucked underneath flaps, and on the passenger side, the hardware is mounted too close to the seat belt.

Cruze Interior Quality

The Cruze has a lovely interior with lots of premium materials. Though some hard plastics still exist, they're high quality and don’t feel out of place. The standard touch-screen display is particularly nice and adds to the cabin's modern styling. For a swankier ride, you can add upgrades like leather seats, a Bose sound system, and automatic climate control.

Cruze Cargo Space

The hatchback provides the most cargo space in the Cruze lineup. Its 22.7-cubic-foot cargo area (47.2 with the rear seats folded) is one of the largest among compact cars. Though the sedan body style has a smaller trunk, its measurements are still above average for this class. Between the 2018 Cruze sedans, the L and LS models have the largest trunks (at 14.8 cubic feet), while the LT and Premier Cruze sedan models have 13.9-cubic-foot trunks. That means you can haul one or two more bags in the back of a Cruze sedan than you can in a Toyota Corolla or Mazda3.

Cruze Infotainment, Bluetooth, and Navigation

The 2018 Cruze not only comes with an extraordinary set of standard features, but the technology itself is impressive as well. All trim levels feature the Chevrolet MyLink infotainment system, an interface that garners praise for its crisp graphics, responsive touch screen, and straightforward menus. The system comes with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, which bring some of your favorite smartphone apps directly to the touch-screen display. A Wi-Fi hot spot, a rearview camera, and Bluetooth are also standard.

For more information, read What Is Apple CarPlay? and What Is Android Auto? Then, see the Best Cars With Apple CarPlay and Best Cars With Android Auto.

Cruze Performance

Cruze Engine: Tuned for Efficiency, Not Speed

The Cruze comes with a turbocharged gasoline engine, and its optional turbodiesel engine is now available in both the sedan and the hatchback models. Both engines come with either a manual or an automatic transmission. Don't plan to engage in any off-the-line sprints with the Cruze, as it takes its time getting up to speed. Commuters will appreciate the refined performance and fuel savings, but spirited drivers may be happier with more athletic alternatives.

Cruze Gas Mileage: Excellent for Commuters

The Cruze has one of the highest fuel economy ratings in its class. Its most fuel-efficient powertrain is the turbodiesel engine, which returns 30 mpg in the city and 52 mpg on the highway. The turbocharged gasoline engine is also quite thrifty. When paired with an automatic transmission, the Cruze sedan gets an EPA-rated 29 mpg in the city and 40 mpg on the highway.

Cruze Ride and Handling: Comfortable Highway Cruiser

The Cruze comes to life on the highway, where its powertrains feel refined and are matched by polished handling. The steering needs only a light touch, and the suspension smooths out rough pavement for a comfortable ride.

Cruze Reliability

Is the Chevrolet Cruze Reliable?

The 2018 Cruze has a great predicted reliability rating of 4.5 out of five from J.D. Power.

Chevrolet Cruze Warranty

Chevrolet covers the Cruze with a three-year/36,000-mile bumper-to-bumper warranty and a five-year/60,000-mile powertrain warranty.

Cruze Safety

Cruze Crash Test Results

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration gives both the Chevrolet Cruze hatchback and sedan a five-star overall safety rating. The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety awards it the highest rating of Good in two crash tests. These scores are respectable, though a few compact cars – like the Toyota Corolla, Mazda3, and Nissan Sentra – have higher safety ratings.

Cruze Safety Features

The Cruze comes standard with a rearview camera and Chevrolet's Teen Driver assist. This built-in system is handy when a young driver (or someone else) borrows your car, as it lets you set limits on the car's maximum speed and the stereo volume and then gives you a report card detailing how the car was driven.

Available safety features include rear parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, lane change alert, lane departure warning, lane keep assist, rear cross traffic alert, forward collision alert, and a following distance indicator (which helps you stay a safe distance behind the vehicle ahead of you).

Which Chevrolet Cruze Model Is Right for Me?

The 2018 Cruze is available as a sedan (which has four trims and a starting price of $16,975) and a hatchback (which starts at $21,320 and is only available in the top two trim levels). The base Cruze comes with a turbocharged four-cylinder engine, a six-speed manual transmission, and front-wheel drive. A six-speed automatic is available in some trims for between $600 and $1,000. Chevrolet also offers a turbodiesel engine for both the sedan and the hatchback, with models priced at $23,820 and $25,520, respectively. This engine comes with either a six-speed manual or a nine-speed automatic transmission.

The base trim is packed with value. Its list of impressive standard technology includes a 7-inch touch screen, enhanced smartphone integration, and a rearview camera. If you want something a touch nicer and can afford to spend around $21,000, we recommend the Chevrolet Cruze LT. This trim gives you convenient extras like controls on the steering wheel and a split-folding rear seat. Chevy also offers a few different packages with the LT, such as the Convenience package, which adds heated front seats, a proximity key, and an eight-way power adjustable driver's seat for an extra $850.

Chevrolet Cruze L

The base Cruze L sedan ($16,975) features a 7-inch touch-screen display, the Chevrolet MyLink infotainment system, a built-in 4G LTE Wi-Fi hot spot, a USB port, Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, four speakers, power windows and doors, keyless entry, air conditioning, a rearview camera, automatic headlights, and Teen Driver assist.

Chevrolet Cruze LS

The $18,525 LS sedan gets upgraded interior items (such as a three-spoke deluxe steering wheel) and gives you the ability to swap out the manual transmission for an optional six-speed automatic for $1,000.

Chevrolet Cruze LT

For the LT trim, you can pick between the sedan and the hatchback. Standard amenities include a six-speaker stereo, satellite radio, steering wheel-mounted audio and phone controls, cruise control, heated side mirrors, a 60/40-split-folding rear seat, and LED daytime running lights. Powertrain options include the turbocharged gasoline engine with either a manual or automatic transmission and the turbodiesel engine with either a manual or an automatic transmission. Prices for the LT range from $20,920 to $25,920.

Chevrolet Cruze Premier

The top-level Premier trim comes with the turbocharged gasoline engine and the six-speed automatic transmission. Extra features in this $23,520 sedan ($24,020 for the hatchback) include leather seats, a leather-wrapped heated steering wheel, heated front seats, an eight-way power-adjustable driver's seat, push-button start, remote car starter, and ambient lighting.

The Final Call

If you're looking to stretch your buying dollar, the Chevrolet Cruze is a solid pick. It has a low starting price, a rich array of standard features, and exceptional fuel economy. The 7-inch touch screen with the Chevrolet MyLink interface is one of the easiest systems to master and includes Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. Beyond the infotainment center, you'll find an attractive cabin, spacious seating for five, and a sizable trunk, along with performance that nicely merges a comfortable ride with mildly sporty handling.

Don’t just take our word for it. Check out comments from some of the reviews that drive our rankings and analysis.

  • "Continuing on are some of the Cruze's core strengths, such as an appealing mix of technology and safety features, a premium-looking cabin, and a ride quality that is both sporty and comfortable. At the same time, though, there are some great small sedans and hatchbacks out right now. The Honda Civic, which was redesigned two years ago, is one of the best small cars on the market. The Hyundai Elantra and Mazda 3 are also compelling alternatives if you want something with more sport or technology. Overall, we like the Cruze but suggest you also check out a few of these top rivals before making a final decision." -- Edmunds
  • "You'll like the Chevy Cruze if you're looking for a quiet, comfortable and fuel-efficient compact sedan or hatchback with available safety technology like blind-spot monitoring and a forward-collision alert system. Road trippers should shop the diesel with 52 highway mpg and over 700 miles of range. … Every 2018 Chevy Cruze is front-wheel drive, so if you're looking for an all-wheel-drive compact, turn your attention to the Subaru Impreza which comes standard with AWD. If dynamic handling is your thing, the Cruze won't satisfy, so check out the sportier Mazda3, Honda Civic and Ford Focus." -- Kelley Blue Book
  • "Between its accommodating cabin, supple suspension, and strong but muted engine, the Cruze offers a lot to recommend it. Oh, and did we mention the trunk is a huge and usefully shaped hold measuring 14 cubic feet? Were it our dough on the line, we'd still choose a Mazda 3 or a Honda Civic, which are more satisfying to drive. But for buyers who are unconcerned with steering feel or knife-edged chassis response, the Cruze is a darned good car." -- Car and Driver (2016)
