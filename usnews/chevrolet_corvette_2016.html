2016 Chevrolet Corvette Overview

Is the 2016 Chevrolet Corvette a Good Used Car?

The Corvette is a fantastic luxury sports car. Unlike many of its classmates, it offers a comfortable ride and a good-sized trunk. However, look elsewhere for a world-class interior.

Used 2016 Chevrolet Corvette Performance and Interior

The Corvette Stingray’s V8 engine produces 455 horsepower and 460 pound-feet of torque, and models with the dual-mode performance exhaust have 5 extra horses. On Stingray models, the Z51 performance package brings suspension and aerodynamic upgrades. For even more muscle, the Z06 trim’s supercharged V8 puts out 650 horsepower and 650 pound-feet of torque. A seven-speed manual transmission comes standard, and an eight-speed automatic is available. The Z07 package upgrades the Z06’s tires, brakes, and aerodynamics, resulting in a zero-to-60 time of 2.95 seconds.

The Corvette is one of the most agile cars on the road, especially when equipped with either of the performance packages. It has a smooth ride, and models with the available Magnetic Selective Ride Control have a rapidly adjusting suspension system that reacts instantaneously to changes in road conditions. Fuel economy is good for a V8-powered luxury sports car: 13-17 mpg in the city and 22-29 mpg on the highway.

Read more about Corvette performance »

Standard amenities include leather upholstery and power-adjustable seats. Some models have heated and ventilated seats, power-adjustable side bolsters, and lumbar support. The stock seats are supportive, but the available competition sport seats have extra side bolstering for improved lateral support around turns. The Corvette’s wide center console cuts into knee room, which may leave taller folks feeling cramped. Although good, the quality of materials falls short of what you’ll find with other brands like Porsche. Like many luxury sports cars, the Corvette has poor rearward visibility. You’ll have a decent amount of cargo space; the Corvette coupe and convertible have a class-leading 15 and 10 cubic feet of trunk space, respectively.

Standard features include a nine-speaker Bose audio system, dual-zone automatic climate control, and push-button start. Although it's occasionally slow to respond to inputs, the standard MyLink system is easy to use and comes with an 8-inch touch screen, Apple CarPlay, and a built-in Wi-Fi hot spot. Coupe models have a removable roof, and convertibles come with a power-folding fabric top.

Used 2016 Chevrolet Corvette Prices

The dealership price of a used 2016 Chevrolet Corvette ranges from about $55,300 for a base model Stingray coupe to around $87,200 for the Z06 convertible. Sale prices vary depending on the vehicle's condition, mileage, features, and location.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Chevrolet Corvette?

J.D. Power and Associates gives the 2016 Chevrolet Corvette a predicted reliability rating of three out of five, which is average compared to others in its class.

Read more about Corvette reliability »

How Safe Is the Corvette?

As with all other 2016 luxury sports cars, the Corvette has not been tested by either the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration or the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety. 

A rearview camera comes standard in the Corvette, and a front curb-view camera and a head-up display are available. Competitors offer features like a driver drowsiness monitor and blind spot monitoring with rear cross traffic alert.

See Corvette safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used Corvette?

The seventh generation of the Corvette began with the 2014 model year and includes the 2017 Chevrolet Corvette. For 2015, the Corvette received an updated automatic transmission and saw the introduction of the Z06 model. Apple CarPlay was added to the standard features list for 2016, and the C7.R Special Edition was introduced. You can save some money by purchasing an older model, which has similar features.

Compare the 2014, 2015, and 2016 Corvette »

Which Used Chevrolet Corvette Is Right for Me?

The 2016 Chevy Corvette’s two models are the Stingray and the Z06. Each offers three trim levels. The Corvette Stingray comes in 1LT, 2LT, and 3LT, and the Z06 comes in 1LZ, 2LZ, and 3LZ. The Z51 performance package is available with any Stingray trim, and the Z07 package is available with any Z06 trim. For most shoppers, the Stingray 2LT is the one to go for. It comes with features that make driving the Corvette easier, like a front curb-view camera and a head-up display, as well as niceties like a 10-speaker Bose audio system, navigation, and heated and ventilated seats.

For added peace of mind, you might also consider a certified pre-owned Corvette. For all of its CPO vehicles, Chevy extends the original new-car powertrain warranty to six years or 100,000 miles and provides a one-year/12,000-mile bumper-to-bumper limited warranty. Chevrolet CPO vehicles must pass a 172-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read Chevy’s warranty page carefully.

2016 Chevrolet Corvette and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 Chevrolet Corvette or 2016 Dodge Viper?

Like the Corvette, the Dodge Viper delivers an adrenaline-charged driving experience, aided by incredible power and razor-sharp handling. But the Corvette is a better choice. The Viper has a harsher ride, worse fuel economy, and a lower predicted reliability rating than the Vette. Also, it doesn’t offer an automatic transmission. The Viper’s loud cabin uses materials that fall short of its rivals, like the Corvette.

Which Is Better: 2016 Chevrolet Corvette or 2016 Nissan GT-R?

The Nissan GT-R’s thrilling power and corner-carving agility make it an appealing alternative to the Corvette. However, unless having the GT-R’s standard all-wheel drive and two barely usable rear seats are top priorities, you're better off with the Corvette. It has a smoother ride, superior fuel economy, and more trunk space. Unlike the Corvette, the Nissan GT-R isn’t available with a manual transmission, a built-in Wi-Fi hot spot, or Apple CarPlay.
