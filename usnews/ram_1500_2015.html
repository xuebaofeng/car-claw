2015 Ram 1500 Overview

Is the 2015 Ram 1500 a Good Used Truck?

The Ram 1500 has an upscale interior, comfortable ride, and powerful lineup of engines. It also won our 2015 Best Full Size Truck for the Money award. However, its lower towing and hauling capability compared to some rivals may make you think twice before making a final purchase.

Used 2015 Ram 1500 Performance and Interior

The 2015 Ram 1500 comes with a 305-horsepower V6. A 240-horsepower EcoDiesel V6 and a 395-horsepower V8 are available. An eight-speed automatic transmission is standard and a six-speed automatic transmission is available.

The base engine provides sufficient power for everyday driving and light-duty work, but the V8 and diesel engines are more capable for towing and hauling. The eight-speed transmission shifts quickly and smoothly.

The base Ram 1500 gets 17 mpg in the city and 25 mpg on the highway, which is average for the class. With the diesel engine, the truck gets 20 mpg in the city and 28 on the highway, which is excellent.

Rear-wheel drive is standard in the Ram 1500 and four-wheel drive is available. Another available feature is the Active Level Four-Corner Air Suspension system, which gives you five modes for different ground clearance heights to enable superior off-roading capabilities. Both the standard suspension and the available air suspension provide a comfortable and composed ride on imperfect road surfaces. The Ram 1500 can tow up to 10,650 pounds and haul up to 1,900 pounds.

Read more about Ram 1500 performance »

On standard vinyl upholstery, Regular Cab models seat up to three and Quad and Crew Cabs seat up to six. Available features include cloth or leather upholstery, heated and ventilated front seats, a heated steering wheel, and a power-adjustable driver's seat. The front seats are comfortable and supportive. Rear-seat legroom is good in the Quad Cab and exceptional in the Crew Cab.

Regular Cab 1500s are outfitted with either a 6-foot-4-inch bed or an 8-foot bed. Quad Cabs are only available with 8-foot beds. Crew Cabs have 5-foot-7-inch or 6-foot-4-inch beds. Lockable, weather-tight storage compartments built into the bed sidewalls are available in all but the 8-foot bed. 

The outboard seats in some 2-row Ram 1500s have a complete system of lower anchors, plus an upper tether on the middle seat. Other Ram 1500s have full sets of LATCH equipment in all three rear seats. 

Standard features include a USB port and a six-speaker stereo. Available features include navigation, satellite radio, a nine-speaker stereo, and the voice-activated Uconnect infotainment system with a 5- or 8.4-inch touch screen, Bluetooth, and smartphone integration.

The touch-screen buttons are large and easy to use, and there are physical buttons for audio and climate adjustments. Uconnect operates well, and the voice command system works better than most competitors'.

The 2015 Ram 1500 has an average predicted reliability score of three out of five from J.D. Power and Associates.

How Safe Is the 1500?

The 2015 Ram 1500 received an overall safety score of four stars out of five stars from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. It received four stars in frontal crash tests and five stars in side crash tests. In rollover tests, four-wheel-drive models earned three stars and rear-wheel-drive models earned four stars. The Ram 1500 earned the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety's highest score of Good in the head restraints and seats, side crash, and moderate overlap front crash tests. It received a Marginal rating in small overlap front crash and roof strength tests.

Standard safety features include hill start assist. Available safety features include a rearview camera and front and rear park assist.

See Ram 1500 safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used Ram 1500?

The 2015 Ram 1500 is the seventh year of the pickup's fourth generation, which launched for 2009 as the Dodge Ram 1500 and includes the 2017 model year. There have been many changes during this generation, including the addition of a new diesel V6 engine for the 2014 model year and a new transmission for the 2013 model year. You may be able to get a similar truck for less money by shopping for a 2013 or 2014 model.

Compare the 2013, 2014, and 2015 Ram 1500 »

Which Used Ram 1500 Is Right for Me?

The 2015 Ram 1500 comes in three body styles: Regular Cab, Quad Cab, and Crew Cab. There are three available bed lengths: 8 feet, 6 feet 4 inches, and 5 feet 7 inches. Then, there are 10 trims: Tradesman, Express, HFE, SLT, Big Horn/Lone Star, Outdoorsman, Sport, Laramie, Laramie Longhorn, and Laramie Limited.

You'll get the best value by shopping for the Big Horn/Lone Star trim. This model comes is available in all body styles with any of the engines. Standard features include keyless entry, and the Uconnect 5.0 infotainment system.

You may want to consider a certified pre-owned vehicle for a longer warranty. For CPO Ram vehicles, the original new-car powertrain warranty is extended to seven years or 100,000 miles. CPO vehicles also get a three-month/3,000-mile Maximum Coverage Care warranty. There are restrictions related to mileage and models, so read the Ram warranty page carefully.

2015 Ram 1500 and Other Trucks to Consider

Which Is Better: 2015 Ram 1500 or 2015 Ford F-150?

With its lower projected cost of ownership, higher tow rating, and better safety scores, there's no doubt the Ford F-150 is the truck to buy. Its lower trim cabin materials may not be as nice as the Ram 1500's, but it's a better all-around truck with well-balanced handling and muscular turbocharged engines.

Which Is Better: 2015 Ram 1500 or 2015 Toyota Tundra?

The Toyota Tundra may have generous rear-seat legroom and good off-roading capabilities, but it has poor fuel economy and a lackluster cabin design with hard plastics. Both trucks' engines produce similar horsepower, but the Ram 1500 is better suited for hauling cargo and towing. Overall, the Ram 1500 is the better truck.

Which Is Better: 2015 Ram 1500 or 2015 Chevrolet Silverado 1500?

The Chevrolet Silverado 1500 has a low projected cost of ownership, above-average reliability, and excellent safety scores. Its standard engine doesn't produce as much horsepower as the Ram 1500's, but its top V8 makes a muscular 420 horsepower. The Silverado can also tow more than the Ram 1500 – up to 12,000 pounds. Add its comfortable handling and responsive steering, and it’s the pickup to buy.
