2016 MINI Cooper Paceman Overview

Is the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman a Good Used Car?

The 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman is a decent used car, but it doesn't look that great when you compare it to other used subcompacts. The Mini Paceman provides a good driving experience thanks to its punchy available engines and agile handling. However, when it comes to ride quality and tech offerings, other small cars are better. The Paceman also trails the competition in more practical measures, like cargo space and fuel economy.

How Much Is the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman?

Based on our site’s listings for the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman, you can expect to pay around $20,500. Your exact price will depend on the condition, mileage, features, and location of the used Paceman you select.

How Much Does the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Cost to Own?

The 2016 Paceman's five-year costs for gas, insurance, repairs, and maintenance are projected to be around $27,000. That's much more than you're likely to pay with other subcompact cars.

Is It Better to Buy a Used or New Paceman?

Mini discontinued the Paceman after the 2016 model year, so you can't buy a newer model. However, there are several highly ranked 2018 subcompact cars that you can buy new for about what a 2016 Paceman would cost you – if not less. These include the Honda Fit, Chevrolet Spark, Toyota Yaris iA, and Kia Rio. Each has a starting price of below $16,500 and gets better fuel economy than the 2016 Paceman. These 2018 subcompacts also incorporate features you can't get in a Paceman, such as a touch-screen infotainment system, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, a rearview camera, and forward collision warning.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman?

The Paceman has a predicted reliability score of 3.5 out of five, which is above average for the industry. However, it's in the middle of the pack for a subcompact car.

2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Recalls

There are currently three safety recalls on the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman: one involves the turn signal accidently disabling the brake light, another involves a steering gearbox malfunction, and another has to do with the passenger-side air bag. If these recalls affect the 2016 Mini Paceman you are considering, make sure the issues are resolved before you buy.

See more information on Mini Cooper Paceman safety recalls »

Which Model Year of the Mini Cooper Paceman Is Best?

The 2016 Mini Paceman marked the model’s final year of production. There weren't any major changes between 2013 – the Paceman’s debut model year – and 2016, so you may want to consider a virtually identical older model to save money. All Paceman models reviewed on our site received comparable overall scores from year to year.

Which Used Mini Cooper Paceman Model Is Right for Me?

The 2016 Paceman is available in three trims, each of which coincides with engine performance: Cooper Paceman (121 horsepower), Cooper S Paceman (181 horsepower), and John Cooper Works Paceman ALL4 (208 horsepower). Standard features include satellite radio, a CD player, Bluetooth, a USB input, air conditioning, and remote keyless entry. Available features include heated front seats, parking sensors, proximity keyless entry, a 6.5-inch infotainment screen, navigation, and a moonroof. When shopping for a used Paceman, look carefully at its options list, as features and cosmetic styling vary widely from vehicle to vehicle.

Certified Pre-Owned Mini Cooper Paceman Warranty

Mini’s certified pre-owned program provides one year of coverage with unlimited miles following the conclusion of the four-year, 50,000-mile Mini New Passenger Car Limited Warranty. Mini CPO vehicle warranties are not transferable on models sold on or after April 1, 2014. Each Mini must pass an inspection to become certified pre-owned. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Mini warranty page carefully. The model's original sale date determines whether it is eligible for the CPO program.

Many brands have CPO programs that are superior to Mini's, including Lexus, Porsche, Mercedes-Benz, Chevrolet, and Honda.

How Safe Is the Paceman?

The 2016 Paceman was not put through crash tests by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. The Paceman is available with rear parking sensors and adaptive headlights that pivot around corners. No other advanced driver assistant features are available, not even a rearview camera.

2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Versus the Competition

Which Is Better: 2016 Mini Paceman or 2016 Mini Countryman?

The 2016 Paceman is a two-door version of the four-door Mini Countryman hatchback. Both share the same engines and similar design cues, as well as common shortcomings like marginal cargo space and few driver assistance features. The five-seat Countryman is the better Mini, however. Its rear seats are more accessible and comfortable, and used models are often found at the same price point as the Paceman.

Which Is Better: 2016 Mini Paceman or 2016 Mini Clubman?

The 2016 Mini Clubman is the most practical option in the Mini lineup, and it’s a better choice overall than the Paceman. The four-door Clubman seats five and has a much roomier cargo area (47.9 cubic feet). Its buttons and infotainment controls are better arranged, and its available features list dwarfs the Paceman’s. Clubman features include an available rearview camera, a head-up display, parallel parking assist, and adaptive cruise control. Both models boast nimble handling and comparable fuel economy.

Which Is Better: 2016 Mini Paceman or 2016 Mini Cooper Hardtop?

The 2016 Mini Cooper Hardtop (available in two- and four-door styles) is a better buy than the Paceman, but just barely. The Mini Cooper boasts a simplified control layout, advanced safety features like adaptive cruise control, and used car prices that are typically much lower than the Paceman’s. However, both cars have a stiff ride over uneven pavement and less cargo space than top rivals.

2016 Paceman Performance

How Does the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Drive?

The 2016 Paceman comes standard with a 121-horsepower four-cylinder engine. It’s paired with a six-speed manual transmission, while a six-speed automatic is available. There are other powertrain options as well. Both are turbocharged four-cylinders, and they put out 181 horsepower (Cooper S trim) and 208 horsepower (John Cooper Works, or JCW, trim).

The base engine feels a bit weak, and the turbo-fours deliver more energetic acceleration. In any setup, the Paceman provides sharp handling. The brakes are strong as well, but the ride is stiffer than in some rivals. Front-wheel drive comes standard, and Mini's ALL4 all-wheel-drive system is available.

Does the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Get Good Gas Mileage?

The Paceman gets below-average fuel economy for the class, at 25 mpg in the city and 29 mpg on the highway with the base engine. With the 181-horsepower turbo-four, it gets 25/31 mpg city/highway. With the 208-horsepower turbo-four, it gets 23/30 mpg city/highway.

2016 Paceman Interior

How Many People Does the 2016 Paceman Seat?

The 2016 Paceman seats four. The front seats are high, which provides a great driving position. The rear seats can fit adults reasonably well, but they can get uncomfortable on longer drives.

How Many Car Seats Fit in the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman?

The Paceman has two full sets of LATCH child-seat connectors on the second-row outboard seats. The setup has not been evaluated for its ease of use.

2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Features

Standard features in the 2016 Mini Paceman include a USB port, Bluetooth, HD Radio, an auxiliary audio input, and push-button start.

Available features include satellite radio, a panoramic sunroof, a Harman Kardon sound system, rear parking sensors, navigation, and the Mini Connected voice-controlled infotainment system with smartphone app integration and a 6.5-inch display.

2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Dimensions

Mini Cooper Paceman Cargo Space

The 2016 Mini Paceman has 11.7 cubic feet of cargo space behind the rear seats and 38.1 cubic feet with the rear seats folded. Those are below-average totals for a subcompact hatchback. Some small sedans have more room in the trunk than the Paceman does behind the rear seats.

2016 Paceman Length and Weight

The 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman is about 13.5 feet long and has a curb weight ranging from 2,767 to 2,930 pounds, depending on trim and configuration.

Where Was the 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman Built?

The 2016 Mini Cooper Paceman was built in Graz, Austria.
