2016 Mazda CX-9 Overview

Is the 2016 Mazda CX-9 a Good Used SUV?

A used 2016 Mazda CX-9 is a good choice for buyers who need a three row midsize SUV but also want great performance. Mazda completely redesigned the CX-9 for this model year; the cabin in upper trims is similar quality to the interiors found in luxury SUVs. 

Used 2016 Mazda CX-9 Performance and Interior

The CX-9 is Mazda's largest vehicle, but it still maintains the spirit and athleticism the brand is known for. Steering is precise through turns, and its suspension keeps body roll to a minimum while also delivering a smooth and comfortable ride. A new turbocharged four-cylinder engine was introduced for 2016 to replace the V6 found in previous CX-9 models. It puts out 227 horsepower with non-premium fuel, or 250 horsepower with premium fuel. Though the 2016 CX-9 doesn't have loads of raw power at high speeds, its excellent torque gets you moving quickly from a stop. However, you may wish for more muscle for passing other cars on the highway. Excellent fuel economy also makes the CX-9 appealing – it gets 22 mpg in the city and 28 mpg on the highway. In models with all-wheel drive, those estimates drop to 21 mpg in the city and 26 on the highway.

Read more about CX-9 performance »

The 2016 Mazda CX-9 seats seven in three rows. First- and second-row comfort is excellent, but the third row drew mixed reviews since it offers less room than the 2015 model. Ideally, it’s a row best left to children. There are two complete LATCH child-seat systems in the second row, as well as an additional tether anchor on the second-row middle seat and the third-row passenger-side seat.

Mazda upgraded the CX-9's infotainment system for 2016, offering a standard 7-inch screen or an optional 8-inch display. You can use the touch screen when the car is stopped, but when in motion, you must control functions through a knob on the console. Sometimes it takes several steps to perform basic commands, but redundant buttons and controls on the dash are easy to see and reach.

Used 2016 Mazda CX-9 Prices

The price of a used 2016 Mazda CX-9 ranges from about $30,200 for the base Sport trim to around $43,100 for the CX-9 Signature with all-wheel drive. However, the vehicle's exact price will vary based on its condition, mileage, features, and location.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Mazda CX-9?

The 2016 CX-9 has a predicted reliability rating of three out of five from J.D. Power and Associates, which is average for the midsize SUV class.

Read more about CX-9 reliability »

How Safe Is the CX-9?

The 2016 Mazda CX-9 did not undergo any crash testing, which hurts its placement in our overall scores and rankings.

A rearview camera is the only standard driver assistance feature, but moving up just one trim level will get you blind spot monitoring and rear cross traffic alert. These features detect cars that are out of your line of sight, which can be a common problem for a big SUV. Other available features include lane keep assist, adaptive cruise control, and automatic emergency braking. The collision prevention systems can be quick to engage, even when no imminent threat exists. While this sensitivity can be adjusted, the settings reset every time the car shuts off.

See CX-9 safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used CX-9?

The 2016 Mazda CX-9 was the first year of the SUV's second generation, which includes the 2017 model year. Significant changes for the redesign included a new four-cylinder engine (which replaced the V6), an updated infotainment system with a larger screen, and better interior quality. However, the 2016 CX-9 has less cargo space and third-row passenger room than the previous model.

The 2017 CX-9 is mostly unchanged from the previous year. That means you’ll likely save money by choosing a used 2016 model – without missing out on the features of the newer model.

Compare the 2016 and 2017 CX-9 »

Which Used Mazda CX-9 Is Right for Me?

The 2016 Mazda CX-9 is available in four trims: Sport, Touring, Grand Touring, and Signature. The standard CX-9 is fairly well-outfitted, with features such as Bluetooth, a rearview camera, and a touch-screen infotainment system. However, you should look for at least a Touring model to get heated and power-adjustable front seats, leather seats, an 8-inch touch screen, and safety features like blind spot monitoring and rear cross traffic alert.

The upper Grand Touring model adds navigation, a head-up display, a Bose stereo, and more advanced safety features, while the top-of-the-line Signature trim gives you Nappa leather and standard all-wheel drive. AWD is optional in all other CX-9 trims.

If you're apprehensive about the unknown history or potential repair costs of a used car, you may want to consider a certified pre-owned model. For each certified pre-owned vehicle, Mazda extends the original limited powertrain warranty to seven years or 100,000 miles and provides a one-year/12,000-mile limited vehicle warranty. To qualify as CPO, each vehicle must pass a 160-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Mazda warranty page carefully.

2016 Mazda CX-9 and Other SUVs to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 Mazda CX-9 or 2016 Mazda CX-5?

The CX-5 is a smaller SUV than the CX-9, so buying one should save you some money. However, the 2016 Mazda CX-5 is a little outdated in comparison; it was fully redesigned for 2017 versus 2016. You won't even get a standard infotainment system in the CX-5. Though the CX-5 only seats five and has less cargo room, its smaller size makes it a little more agile and sporty. The CX-9 is more upscale inside – especially in upper trims – but the CX-5 gets commendable safety scores. Both models are excellent; your choice just depends on your budget and the amount of seats you need.

Which Is Better: 2016 Mazda CX-9 or 2016 Honda Pilot?

The 2016 Honda Pilot is a better choice than the CX-9, especially if space is your priority. It can seat up to eight passengers and even has enough room for adults in the third row. The Pilot also beats out the CX-9 in cargo volume. While both cars feature an upscale interior, the Pilot's infotainment setup can be frustrating. Thanks to its V6 engine, the Pilot is livelier than the CX-9 when you need the most power, such as when passing other cars on the highway.

Which Is Better: 2016 Mazda CX-9 or 2016 Toyota Highlander?

The 2016 Toyota Highlander is a better vehicle than the CX-9 because of its practicality, safety, and reliability. Like the Pilot, the Highlander can seat eight, but the third row is best left to kids (as in the CX-9). You'll get more cargo room in the Highlander than the CX-9, and its standard infotainment system is easy to use. The Highlander is slightly above the CX-9 and the class average for reliability, and it has good safety scores.
