2016 Jaguar F-Type Overview

Is the 2016 Jaguar F-Type a Good Used Car?

The 2016 F-Type is an excellent option in the luxury sports car class. It pairs incredible performance with a luxurious, feature-filled cabin. Its predicted reliability rating, while average, is lower than many rivals' ratings, and it has one of the highest total cost of ownership estimates in the class, which contributes to its low position in our rankings.

Used 2016 Jaguar F-Type Performance and Interior

The F-Type is a thrill to drive, with nimble handling and potent engines. Base and F-Type S models have supercharged V6 engines, and the F-Type R has a supercharged V8. Regardless of which engine you choose, you'll find the F-Type is very quick. A six-speed manual is standard in rear-wheel-drive V6 models, while a slick-shifting eight-speed automatic is available. The automatic is standard with all-wheel drive and V8 models. The F-Type tackles corners with ease, staying level and composed all the way through. The ride can be uncomfortable on rough roads, like many sports cars. The downside to the F-Type's exceptional performance is poor fuel economy, even compared to other luxury sports cars. The F-Type gets up to 19 mpg in the city and 27 mpg on the highway.

Read more about F-Type performance »

The F-Type is a sports car through and through, but it doesn't neglect its luxury badge inside. The cabin has high-quality materials and clean, modern styling. The seats are a bit of a mixed bag. They are supportive and have plenty of headroom, but they aren't comfortable for long trips, and legroom is limited.

The F-Type also has a well-equipped interior. It has a long list of standard features, including an 8-inch touch-screen infotainment system, navigation, and a Meridian premium audio system. Overall, the infotainment system is user-friendly and has intuitive menus, but it can be slow to respond to inputs. There are physical buttons and knobs for some climate and audio settings, but some adjustments require you to dig into a menu on the touch screen, which can be cumbersome. Like most sports cars, the F-Type has very little cargo space.

Read more about F-Type interior »

Used 2016 Jaguar F-Type Prices

Prices for a used 2016 F-Type range from around $58,300 for the base coupe model up to around $94,400 for the F-Type R convertible. Pricing can vary with vehicle condition, mileage, and location.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Jaguar F-Type?

The F-Type earns a predicted reliability rating of three out of five, which is a bit below average for a luxury sports car. Still, three is considered average compared to all vehicles.

Read more about F-Type reliability »

How Safe Is the F-Type?

Like most luxury sports cars, the F-Type hasn't been crash tested by the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety or the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration.

A few advanced safety features are available in the F-Type, including a rearview camera, blind spot monitoring, rear cross traffic alert, and front and rear parking sensors.

See F-Type safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used F-Type?

The 2016 F-Type is part of the luxury sport car's first generation, which debuted with the 2014 model and includes the 2018 model. There are a few differences between the model years. The 2014 F-Type was only available as a convertible, and the range-topping F-Type R trim was not yet available. The 2015 model added the F-Type R trim, as well as a coupe body style. For 2016, a six-speed manual transmission and available all-wheel drive were added.

Compare the 2014, 2015, and 2016 F-Type »

Which Used Jaguar F-Type Is Right for Me?

The F-Type is available in three trims: base, S, and R. You can choose from either coupe or convertible body styles. Higher trims add few extra features; the main differences are performance related. You'll get plenty of thrills from the base model's supercharged V6 engine, but if you have the need for speed, the F-Type S and F-Type R trims have more power. Base trims only have rear-wheel drive. The S trim is available with all-wheel drive, and the R only comes with all-wheel drive.

When you're ready to buy a used F-Type, consider a certified pre-owned model at a Jaguar dealership. You'll pay more than you would for a non-CPO F-Type, but the cost may be worth it to you. For its certified pre-owned vehicles, Jaguar extends the original new-car warranty to seven years or 100,000 miles. Every CPO Jaguar must pass a 165-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Jaguar warranty page carefully.

Read more about certified pre-owned vehicles »

2016 Jaguar F-Type and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 Jaguar F-Type or 2016 Porsche Boxster?

If you’re considering the F-Type, the convertible Porsche Boxster is also worth a test drive. Its engines may not produce as much horsepower as the F-Type's, but the Boxster certainly doesn't feel slow. Its real high point is its exceptional handling. Few sports cars come close to matching the Boxster's balance and poise, which help it carve corners at high speeds. The Boxster earns one of the highest predicted reliability ratings in the class, and with the base engine, it gets better fuel economy than the F-Type. If you don't like having the wind in your hair, consider the Porsche Cayman, which is essentially a coupe version of the Boxster.

Which Is Better: 2016 Jaguar F-Type or 2016 Alfa Romeo 4C?

If performance is the only factor in your decision-making process, the Alfa Romeo 4C may be the car for you. It's constructed out of carbon fiber, so it's incredibly lightweight. Even though it only has a turbocharged four-cylinder engine, the 4C is shockingly fast, and it earns better-than-average fuel economy. The 4C grips the road around corners, and its standard Brembo brakes can bring it to a stop in a hurry. There are some serious sacrifices for the 4C's performance, however. The cabin has very few features and lacks the luxury appeal of the F-Type. The 4C also has even less cargo space than the F-Type.
