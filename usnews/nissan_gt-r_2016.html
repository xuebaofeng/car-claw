2016 Nissan GT-R Overview

Is the 2016 Nissan GT-R a Good Used Car?

The GT-R is a beast that will blow the doors off most other cars on the road. However, for the price, you might think it's a bit lacking in the luxury department. Many class rivals have more upscale interiors and longer features lists.

Used 2016 Nissan GT-R Performance and Interior

In the performance realm, this Nissan supercar offers a meaty engine and superb handling. Its base twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces 545 horsepower and blisteringly quick acceleration, and the NISMO edition is worthy of bearing the GT-R's nickname – Godzilla. This version features a 600-horsepower V6 with high-capacity twin turbochargers. The GT-R has a top speed of 196 mph and can go from zero to 60 mph in just 3 seconds. Take it out on a winding road or a track, and you'll be impressed by how well it handles corners, with standard all-wheel drive imparting excellent road grip. It's not quite as thirsty as an Audi R8, but you'll spend more to fuel a GT-R than a BMW i8 or Porsche 911. The Nissan GT-R has a fuel economy rating of 16 mpg in the city and 22 mpg on the highway.

Read more about GT-R performance »

The GT-R seats four people. It is one of only a handful of cars in the luxury sports car class – along with the Porsche 911, BMW 4 Series, and Audi TT – that seats more than two. The front seats are comfortable for long trips, and the large doors make it easy to climb into and out of the car. As with many sports cars, the rear seats are too cramped to comfortably accommodate adults, but this area is helpful to store personal belongings in the cabin. Each back seat has a full set of lower and upper anchors for securing child safety seats. The trunk has enough space for a couple of golf bags or a weekend’s worth of luggage, but its high liftover height and narrow opening can make it difficult to load your items.

Although the Nissan GT-R uses excellent materials for its sharply designed interior, it can't compete with most luxury rivals. The GT-R’s interior is better suited to someone who's looking for a driver's car, not ornamentation. There's an infotainment system that was inspired by the video game "Gran Turismo" and includes a 7-inch touch screen, voice recognition, and navigation. It provides performance information and other vehicular data, but other than the usual audio and climate functions and a few smartphone connectivity and entertainment features, there’s not much else.

Used 2016 Nissan GT-R Prices

The price of a used 2016 Nissan GT-R ranges from about $93,800 for the Premium trim with a 545-horsepower V6 to around $138,200 for the NISMO trim with a 600-horsepower V6. The price varies depending on the vehicle's condition, mileage, features, and location.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Nissan GT-R?

In comparison to other cars, the 2016 GT-R scores slightly above average in predicted reliability. However, other sporty cars – including the Porsche 911 and BMW 4 Series – will likely be more dependable.

Read more about GT-R reliability »

How Safe Is the GT-R?

No crash tests have been performed on the 2016 GT-R. This is not out of the ordinary for a luxury sports car. The GT-R has a standard rearview camera, but it does not offer much else in the way of safety or driver assistance features. Buyers interested in features like blind spot monitoring, lane departure warning, or adaptive cruise control will have to look at class rivals like the BMW 4 Series and Jaguar F-Type.

See GT-R safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used GT-R?

In 2009, Nissan launched the GT-R as its grand touring and racing coupe. The car has received numerous updates over the years (including a styling refresh and performance improvements for the 2017 GT-R), but it is still the same basic car as when it debuted. Not all trims are available each year (the 2015 Nissan GT-R Track Edition and 2016 GT-R 45th Anniversary Gold Edition are among the short-range standouts). Unless you are specifically seeking out one of these special trims, you may be able to save some money by shopping for an older model year.

Compare the 2015, 2016, and 2017 GT-R »

Which Used Nissan GT-R Is Right for Me?

Of the 2016 GT-R's four available trims, the Premium and the Black Edition are the two you'll most likely be comparing. Aesthetics are all that separate these two, with the Black Edition boasting a carbon fiber rear spoiler, leather-trimmed Recaro sport seats in the front, and red accents throughout the cabin. For supercar shoppers looking to make a bolder statement, the NISMO trim delivers extreme performance (for a higher price). The 45th Anniversary Gold Edition is extremely rare; only 30 were built for the U.S.

You might consider a certified pre-owned GT-R. For its certified pre-owned vehicles, Nissan extends the original new-car warranty to seven years or 100,000 miles. To become certified pre-owned, each vehicle must pass a 167-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Nissan warranty page carefully.

2016 Nissan GT-R and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 Nissan GT-R or 2016 Porsche 911?

In terms of performance, the Nissan GT-R is one of the few vehicles that's in the same ballpark as the Porsche 911 – and it can certainly give the German carmaker a run for its money. The 911 seats four, but unlike the GT-R, you and your passengers will be bathed in luxury and surrounded by high-grade materials. The 911 is also predicted to be one of the most reliable sports cars you can buy.

Which Is Better: 2016 Nissan GT-R or 2015 Audi R8?

2015 was the last year of the Audi R8's first generation, after which the supercar took a short break before its relaunch for the 2017 model year. Its price tag falls within close quarters of the GT-R’s, as do its performance specs. The two-seat R8 doesn't work quite as a well as a daily driver, but if you’re after the occasional hell-raising, both the Audi and the Nissan are exemplary picks.

Which Is Better: 2016 Nissan GT-R or 2016 Mercedes-Benz SL?

The Mercedes-Benz SL is a tamer touring sports car … unless you are pulling up in an AMG edition. The Mercedes-AMG SL63's twin-turbo V8 has 577 horsepower and can propel you to 60 mph in 4.1 seconds. If that's not fast enough, the AMG SL65 hits 60 mph in 3.9 seconds, thanks to its 621-horsepower twin-turbo V12. With either one, you'll be riding in the lap of luxury, with a retractable hardtop so you can feel the sun on your face.
