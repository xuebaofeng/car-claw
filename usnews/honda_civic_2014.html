2014 Honda Civic Overview

Is the 2014 Honda Civic a Good Used Car?

If you want a used compact car that’s comfortable for daily driving, you can’t go wrong with the Civic. It offers more passenger space than most rivals, an upscale cabin, and a smooth ride. Additionally, the Civic's reliability score is unmatched among compact competitors.

Powering the Civic is a four-cylinder engine that delivers sufficient punch. Quick acceleration comes from either the standard five-speed manual transmission or the available automatic transmission (CVT). Both transmissions are refined and make good use of the four-cylinder's power. The performance-oriented Civic Si comes with a larger, more powerful four-cylinder and a manual transmission. The Civic is not particularly agile; its handling is more comfortable and sedate. All Civics come with front-wheel drive. With the base engine and the automatic transmission, the Civic gets up to 29 mpg in the city and 37 mpg on the highway, which are above-average estimates for the class.

Read more about Civic performance »

There are some plastic surfaces inside the Civic, but most are nicely textured. The cabin is very quiet, too, thanks to lots of sound-absorbing materials. The driver and front passenger will be comfortable on the supportive seats, and sedan models have plenty of head- and legroom in both the front and the back. The LATCH child-seat system’s lower anchors are easy to access, but the upper tether anchors in coupe models are hard to reach due to the sloping roofline.

The Civic is well-equipped with standard features like Bluetooth, a USB port, an auxiliary audio input, and a rearview camera. Most rival compact cars do not offer a rearview camera as standard. Some Civic models include a 7-inch touch-screen infotainment system, automatic climate control, a moonroof, and a passenger-side blind spot camera. Touch-sensitive buttons on the center stack control some functions. These buttons are not as easy to use as conventional controls. On the other hand, the infotainment system is straightforward, and it's easy to connect your phone using Bluetooth. The Civic has less trunk space than many rivals.

Used 2014 Honda Civic Prices

Prices for a used 2014 Civic range from about $13,000 for the base LX model to around $18,000 for the SI trim with navigation. The vehicle's condition, location, and mileage can impact pricing.

How Reliable Is the 2014 Honda Civic?

No other compact car can match the Civic's reliability score of 4.5 out of five from J.D. Power and Associates. Most rivals earn a score of three out of five.

How Safe Is the Civic?

The Civic has high safety scores, which is typical for the class. It holds a Top Safety Pick+ designation from the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety and a five-star overall safety rating from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. In NHTSA front and rollover crash tests, the Civic earned four out of five stars.

See Civic safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used Civic?

The 2014 Civic is part of a generation that began with the 2012 model year and ended with the 2015 model year. The 2012, 2013, and 2014 models have minor differences. Every 2013 and 2014 Civic has standard Bluetooth, a USB port, a rearview camera, and air conditioning. These features were optional for the 2012 model year. For 2012 and 2013, a five-speed automatic was available, which was replaced by a CVT for 2014. A touch-screen display and passenger-side blind spot camera were not available in 2012 or 2013 Civics.

Compare the 2012, 2013, and 2014 Civic »

Which Used Honda Civic Is Right for Me?

The 2014 Civic sedan and coupe are available in four trims: LX, EX, EX-L, and Si. The sedan is also available in a fuel-efficient HF trim. Most buyers should be satisfied with the features list in the base LX model, which includes a four-speaker audio system, a 5-inch display screen, Bluetooth, and a USB port. Still, if you can find an EX trim that doesn’t break the bank, it will be a better buy. EX models come with a moonroof, push-button start, a passenger-side blind spot camera, and an upgraded infotainment system with a 7-inch display.

The EX-L trim adds leather upholstery, heated front seats, and a power-adjustable driver's seat. The performance-oriented Si adds most of the features from the EX trim along with a more powerful engine and other performance features. The HF sedan pairs many of the LX's features with low-rolling resistance tires and aerodynamic tweaks to achieve better fuel economy. The Civic is also available as a hybrid, which is reviewed separately.

Select Civic models at Honda dealerships may be covered by a certified pre-owned program. Honda extends its original new-car limited powertrain warranty to seven years or 100,000 miles for all certified pre-owned models, and it extends the new-car non-powertrain warranty to four years or 48,000 miles. If the CPO Honda you purchase is more than four years old or has more than 48,000 miles, you’ll receive a one-year/12,000-mile non-powertrain limited warranty. Honda CPO vehicles must pass a 182-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read Honda’s warranty page carefully.

2014 Honda Civic and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2014 Honda Civic or 2014 Honda Accord?

Unless you need more cargo and passenger space, you'll be better off sticking with the Civic than stepping up to the larger Honda Accord. The Accord earns an average reliability rating that can't match the Civic's top-tier reliability score. The Accord has sportier handling, but at the cost of ride comfort. Otherwise, both Hondas have comparable features, interior designs, and fuel economy estimates.

Which Is Better: 2014 Honda Civic or 2014 Toyota Camry?

Both the Civic and the Toyota Camry earn very high reliability ratings. The Camry offers more space but costs about the same. If you want a little more power, search for a used Camry with the available V6. The Camry’s center stack has traditional buttons and knobs instead of touch-sensitive controls like the Civic. However, the Civic’s infotainment system is easier to use than the system found in some Camrys. The Civic also gets slightly better fuel economy estimates than the Camry.

Which Is Better: 2014 Honda Civic or 2014 Mazda Mazda3?

The Mazda3 is fun to drive when you want some thrills – providing sharp handling and smooth acceleration – but it still has an upscale, feature-filled cabin to keep you and your passengers comfortable and happy on the daily commute. This combination makes it one of the best options in the compact car class. In fact, it won our 2014 Best Compact Car for the Money and Best Compact Car for Families awards. The Mazda3 earns higher crash test scores than the Civic, though it does fall short of the Honda in reliability.
