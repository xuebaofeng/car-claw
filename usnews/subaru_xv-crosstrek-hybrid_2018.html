2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid Overview

Is the 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid a Good Used SUV?

The 2015 XV Crosstrek Hybrid is a good used subcompact SUV. It is fuel-efficient, has a composed ride, and provides a comfortable cabin. It gets great crash test ratings, too. However, the XV Crosstrek Hybrid has less cargo space than many competitors, and its predicted reliability rating is subpar for the class.

Used 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid Performance and Interior

A four-cylinder engine and an electric motor power the XV Crosstrek Hybrid. The pairing gives you quick acceleration from a stop and adequate power on the highway. A continuously variable automatic transmission comes standard, and it feels smooth while letting you manually shift through simulated gears. The XV Crosstrek Hybrid gets up to 29 mpg in the city and 33 mpg on the highway, which is good for the class. The gas-powered XV Crosstrek, which is reviewed separately, gets nearly the same fuel economy. All-wheel drive comes standard. The XV Crosstrek Hybrid has stable, composed handling, but it is not exactly thrilling to drive.

Read more about XV Crosstrek Hybrid performance »

Though it's not especially luxurious, the XV Crosstrek Hybrid's cabin is solidly built with soft-touch materials and simple styling. The cabin is quiet thanks to ample insulation. There's plenty of space for adults in the front seats, while tall passengers in the cramped rear seats will only be comfortable during short trips. All models come standard with heated seats. There's a full set of LATCH child-seat connectors on each of the outboard rear seats, while the middle seat has an additional upper tether anchor.

The base XV Crosstrek Hybrid has a standard Starlink 6.2-inch touch-screen infotainment system, along with Bluetooth, a six-speaker audio system, and push-button start. An upgraded 7-inch touch screen with navigation and satellite radio is available. The physical buttons and knobs on the center stack are user-friendly, but the navigation system can be complicated to use. The radio has poor sound quality and may leave you wishing for an upgraded audio system. Unfortunately, the XV Crosstrek Hybrid doesn't have any premium audio systems available; many rivals offer systems from Bose or other audio companies. It also has less overall cargo room than many rival SUVs.

Used 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid Prices

Prices for the 2015 XV Crosstrek Hybrid start around $22,000 for the base model and climb up to about $25,000 for the Touring trim level. Vehicle condition, mileage, and location can all impact price.

How Reliable Is the 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid?

The XV Crosstrek Hybrid earns a predicted reliability rating of 2.5 out of five from J.D. Power and Associates, which is below average for the class and the industry as a whole.

Read more about XV Crosstrek Hybrid reliability »

How Safe Is the XV Crosstrek Hybrid?

The XV Crosstrek Hybrid performed very well in crash tests. The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety named the XV Crosstrek Hybrid a Top Safety Pick+ and gave it a Good rating – the highest offered – in every crash test category. The XV Crosstrek Hybrid earned a perfect five-star overall rating in National Highway Traffic Safety Administration crash tests, with five stars in the side crash category and four stars in front and rollover crash categories. Available features include a rearview camera and Subaru's EyeSight safety suite, which includes more advanced safety systems like forward collision warning and lane departure warning.

See XV Crosstrek Hybrid safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used XV Crosstrek Hybrid?

The XV Crosstrek Hybrid was introduced as an all-new model for the 2014 model year. For 2015, Subaru updated the infotainment system and added it as standard in all models. Subaru dropped "XV" from the Crosstrek Hybrid’s name for the 2016 model year, in addition to adding available blind spot monitoring and rear cross traffic alert. The 2016 model year was the final model year. If you don't want an infotainment system, consider buying a 2014 model to save some money.

Compare the 2014, 2015, and 2016 XV Crosstrek Hybrid »

Which Used Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid Is Right for Me?

Since there's only a couple thousand dollars difference between the base model and the Touring trim, spring for the top model if you can. It adds an upgraded infotainment system, navigation, satellite radio, leather upholstery, and a power moonroof.

For its certified pre-owned vehicles, Subaru extends the original new-car powertrain warranty to seven years or 100,000 miles. To become certified pre-owned, each vehicle must pass a 152-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Subaru warranty page carefully.

2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid and Other Vehicles to Consider

Which Is Better: 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid or 2015 Honda CR-V?

The Honda CR-V – our 2015 Best Compact SUV for the Money and 2015 Best Compact SUV for Families – is a better option than the XV Crosstrek Hybrid. Even though the CR-V is a nonhybrid and larger than the subcompact Subaru, the Honda gets almost identical fuel economy. The CR-V has more cargo and passenger room, and its upscale cabin has loads of features. There’s also a peppy engine under the hood. The CR-V gets significantly better predicted reliability ratings, and the base model is several thousand dollars cheaper than the base XV Crosstrek Hybrid.

Which Is Better: 2015 Subaru XV Crosstrek Hybrid or 2015 Toyota Prius V?

As long as you don't need all-wheel drive or an SUV-like ride height, the Toyota Prius V is likely a better hybrid for you than the XV Crosstrek Hybrid. The Prius V is a wagon, while the XV Crosstrek Hybrid is a subcompact SUV. It gets even better fuel economy than the Subaru, and its generous passenger and cargo space easily best the XV Crosstrek Hybrid’s. The Prius V also earns higher predicted reliability and safety ratings.
