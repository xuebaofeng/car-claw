2018 Lexus RX 350 Review

by Bryan Siwik | August 3, 2018

The 2018 Lexus RX finishes in the top third of our luxury midsize SUV rankings. It delivers respectable performance and comes with plenty of standard features, including a slew of active safety technology.

Is the Lexus RX 350 a Good SUV?

Yes, the Lexus RX 350 is a good SUV. The RX has a long list of features and ample passenger space, though its cargo hold is somewhat undersized. The standard V6 engine delivers plenty of power, and the RX rides smoothly even over rough pavement.

Should I Buy the Lexus RX 350?

We named the Lexus RX our 2018 Best Luxury 2-Row SUV for the Money for having the best combination of quality and value in the class. It has a lower price than many competitors and is highly practical, making it a great choice as a commuter vehicle. Still, top competitors like the Audi Q7 and Acura MDX are worth considering as well.

Should I Buy a New or Used Lexus RX 350?

There have been notable changes to the Lexus RX 350 every year since its redesign for 2016. The 2017 RX 350 added the Lexus Safety system to its list of standard features (previously optional), which includes forward collision warning with brake assist, adaptive cruise control, and more. For 2018, Lexus added a 3-row model, the RX L, to the RX 350 lineup. 

You can potentially save thousands of dollars by shopping for a used RX 350 instead of a new one, though you should be aware that older models may not have as many features (especially if you buy a pre-2016 model). If you're interested in a used RX 350, be sure to visit our overviews of the 2016 Lexus RX 350 and 2017 Lexus RX 350. Also, check out our Used Car Deals page to learn about savings and discounts on used vehicles.

How Much Does the Lexus RX 350 Cost?

The 2018 Lexus RX starts at $43,270. The all-new RX L model starts at $47,670, while the performance RX F Sport retails for $48,920. Front-wheel drive is standard in all these models, and adding all-wheel drive bumps the base price up by $1,400.

Lexus RX 350 Versus the Competition

Which Is Better: Lexus RX 350 or Acura MDX?

The Acura MDX and Lexus RX 350 are both top picks in this class. The MDX has a slightly higher price than the RX, but it's still less expensive than many competitors. The MDX seats up to seven in its standard configuration, though its third row feels too cramped for adults. It also has a higher maximum cargo capacity. These SUVs are similar in terms of power, fuel economy, and standard safety features, but the MDX is slightly nimbler. On the other hand, the MDX can’t match the higher predicted reliability rating of the Lexus.

Which Is Better: Lexus RX 350 or Audi Q5?

The fully redesigned 2018 Audi Q5 ranks near the top of the highly competitive luxury compact SUV segment. The Q5 provides ample passengers room for five and has more cargo space than the Lexus. The Q5's interior quality is off the charts, and it has a lengthy standard features list. The Audi is pretty fun to drive, and all-wheel drive comes standard. On top of all these positives, the Q5 has a lower base price than the RX 350, making it the better value.

RX 350 Interior

How Many People Does the RX 350 Seat?

The Lexus RX crossover seats five people, while the RX L model seats up to seven. The RX features standard leatherette upholstery, along with 10-way power-adjustable front seats. You can also opt for real leather seats, heated and ventilated front seats, heated, power-folding rear seats, or second-row captain's chairs in the RX L.

The plush front seats provide all-day comfort, but the extra-supportive sport seats in the RX F Sport model might be a bit tight for larger drivers. Passengers in the second row will find plenty of leg- and headroom, which you can increase with the standard sliding and reclining back seat.

RX 350 and Car Seats

The RX has two complete sets of LATCH child-seat connectors in the back seat, and the middle seat can borrow lower anchors from either side. For its ease of use, the LATCH system earns the highest rating of Good+ from the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety. The lower anchors are easily visible in the seat cushion, so it's easy to reach them and attach the strap. The three upper anchors, located on the back of the rear seats, are clearly marked, and there's no other equipment that you may confuse for the anchors.

RX 350 Interior Quality

Though the Lexus RX costs less than many other luxury midsize rivals, you won't sacrifice luxury quality. The RX's solidly built cabin is awash in soft-touch materials, though you may notice some familiar midrange Toyota buttons and knobs. The interior features a minimalist design, especially compared to rivals like the elegant Volvo XC90 and Mercedes-Benz GLE-Class.

RX 350 Cargo Space

The 2018 RX has 18.4 cubic feet of cargo space with the seats up and 56.3 cubic feet with the second-row seats folded. Though you'll have enough room for the whole family to haul their suitcases for vacation, other SUVs offer much more utility. Three-row midsize crossovers like the Acura MDX (68.4 cubic feet max) are more cavernous, as are some luxury compact SUVs like the Audi Q5. The Q5 has 26.8 cubic feet behind its rear seats and 60.4 cubic feet with the seats folded.

A power tailgate is optional, and paired with proximity key entry, you can open it with a wave of your hand when you're near the trunk. However, the back seat doesn't fold completely flat, limiting your ability to carry big or bulky cargo.

RX 350 Infotainment, Bluetooth, and Navigation

The RX offers nearly all the modern conveniences you would expect from a luxury vehicle. Standard features include dual-zone automatic climate control, proximity key entry, push-button start, two USB ports, voice recognition, Bluetooth, HD Radio, and satellite radio. The base infotainment system has an 8-inch display monitor that's intuitively controlled via a center console-mounted control knob. An optional infotainment setup features a 12.3-inch display screen, navigation, and the Enform app suite, but it comes with a big caveat. This interface is managed by the Remote Touch controller, which feels like a small joystick. It's difficult to navigate the screen with it, especially when driving.

Other optional features include a panoramic glass moonroof, a 12-speaker stereo system, a 15-speaker Mark Levinson surround-sound system, a head-up display, a 120-volt outlet, and a rear-seat DVD entertainment system.

Despite its wealth of high-tech features, the RX doesn't provide support for smartphone integration via Android Auto or Apple CarPlay. The upgraded infotainment system includes the Enform app suite, which uses its own interface. Many luxury brands offer the more familiar Apple or Android interface, which allows you to access your phone's content (apps, contacts, media, etc.) directly on the screen. For more information, read What Is Apple CarPlay? and What Is Android Auto? Then, see the Best Cars With Apple CarPlay and Best Cars With Android Auto.

RX 350 Performance

RX 350 Engine: Solid V6 Power

The 2018 Lexus RX comes standard with a hefty 3.5-liter V6 engine, which is good for 295 horsepower. Hit the gas, and the engine gets the RX moving effortlessly. The SUV's eight-speed automatic transmission makes the most of the engine's power, with quick shifts that easily find the right gear for the situation. Class rivals with V6 engines have similar amounts of horsepower, but some luxury SUVs are available with more powerful turbocharged engines.

RX 350 Gas Mileage: Good for the Class

The 2018 RX 350 gets an EPA-estimated 20 mpg in the city and 27 mpg on the highway. The seven-seat RX L model achieves 19/26 mpg city/highway. Adding all-wheel drive drops both ratings by 1 mpg. The RX's fuel efficiency is high for the class, so you don't need to worry about sacrificing good engine performance for good gas mileage.

The Lexus RX Hybrid, which we review separately, gets some of the highest fuel economy estimates in the class: 31 mpg in the city and 28 mpg on the highway (2017 figures). However, you would only save about $100 per year in fuel costs by choosing it over the gas-only model. The RX Hybrid is around $10,000 more than the nonhybrid RX, but it comes standard with all-wheel drive and some other upgrades, so you'll need to figure out if the extra cost is worth it.

RX 350 Ride and Handling: Soft but Not Sporty

The RX prioritizes ride comfort over an engaging drive. The cabin isolates most road and wind noise, while the suspension soaks up uneven pavement. However, there's little steering feel, and handling is generally dull. The RX F Sport comes with an adaptive suspension and adds a Sport S+ driving mode that tunes the SUV for better handling and response.

RX 350 Reliability

Is the Lexus RX 350 Reliable?

The 2018 RX 350 has an above-average predicted reliability rating of 3.5 out of five from J.D. Power

Lexus RX 350 Warranty

The 2018 Lexus RX 350 comes with a four-year/50,000-mile basic warranty and a six-year/70,000-mile powertrain warranty. The Acura MDX has the same coverage. Many luxury vehicles, like the Audi Q5, come with the same basic warranty terms as the RX but offer no additional powertrain coverage.

RX 350 Safety

RX 350 Crash Test Results

The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety named the Lexus RX 350 a 2018 Top Safety Pick. The model received the agency's highest rating of Good in all crash tests. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration gave the RX 350 a perfect five-star rating for side impact safety, as well as four stars for front and rollover crash safety. Front-wheel-drive models received a four-star overall safety rating; all-wheel-drive models earned a five-star overall rating.

RX 350 Safety Features

The Lexus RX comes well-equipped with a bounty of driver assistance and advanced safety features. A rearview camera, automatic high beams, rain-sensing windshield wipers, adaptive cruise control, forward collision warning with brake assist, pedestrian detection, lane departure warning, and lane keep assist are all standard. Optional features include parking sensors, a surround-view camera, blind spot monitoring, rear cross traffic alert, and a head-up display.

Which Lexus RX 350 Model Is Right for Me?

The 2018 Lexus RX is available in three trims: base, the three-row L model, and F Sport. All models come with a V6 engine, an automatic transmission, and front-wheel drive; all-wheel drive is available. Though the base model comes loaded with many features – including several driver assistance features –you'll want to add the Luxury package, which upgrades the standard leatherette seats to real leather and includes heated and ventilated front seats and a panoramic glass roof. Upgrading the infotainment system is a double-edged sword. The standard interface with an 8-inch screen is user-friendly, but if you upgrade for the navigation and larger screen, you'll have to deal with the frustrating Remote Touch controls. Your best bet is to test the system to see if you can live with it.

The RX is also available as a hybrid, which we review separately. The RX Hybrid offers only marginal fuel savings over the nonhybrid model, and it's hard to justify given the much higher price tag.

Lexus RX 350

The 2018 Lexus RX 350 comes standard with leatherette upholstery, 10-way power-adjustable front seats, dual-zone automatic climate control, proximity key entry, push-button start, two USB ports, voice recognition, Bluetooth, HD Radio, satellite radio, an 8-inch infotainment display, a rearview camera, automatic high beams, rain-sensing windshield wipers, adaptive cruise control, forward collision warning, pedestrian detection, lane departure warning, and lane keep assist.

There are several packages and standalone options for both the base and F Sport model. A Luxury package includes a panoramic glass roof, heated and ventilated front seats, a heated wood steering wheel, and semi-aniline leather seats. The Navigation package adds navigation (obviously), a 12-speaker stereo, the Enform app suite, a 12.3-inch screen, and the Remote Touch controller. The Rear Seat Entertainment package comes with a DVD system and a 120-volt power outlet. Additional options include a Mark Levinson audio system, a head-up display, a power liftgate, parking sensors, a surround-view camera, blind spot monitoring, and rear cross traffic alert.

Lexus RX 350 L

The RX 350 L seats up to seven people in three rows. Additional conveniences for this model include power-folding third row seats, third-row dedicated climate controls, and a power tailgate.

Lexus RX 350 F Sport

The Lexus RX 350 with F Sport package adds an adaptive variable suspension, a heated and leather-wrapped steering wheel, heated and ventilated front sport seats, paddle shifters, and upgraded interior and exterior styling.

The Final Call

The Lexus RX is a fine luxury midsize SUV with a low price for the class. It won't wow you with its performance, but it doesn't intend to. Likewise, if you want a more luxurious interior, you'll probably have to go with a pricier competitor.

Don’t just take our word for it. Check out comments from some of the reviews that drive our rankings and analysis.

  • "No, I would not buy a Lexus RX. First, I'm not a big fan of the design. Second, I'm not crazy about how it drives. Third, I don't consider it to be an especially good value." -- New York Daily News
  • "Comfort, quality and a carlike driving experience are key strengths of the 2017 Lexus RX 350. It falls short of its competitors in some ways, but it's still a smart pick if you're looking for a well-rounded luxury crossover." -- Edmunds (2017)
  • "… the Lexus RX 350 luxury SUV continues to give customers the exact blend of performance and comfort they've come to expect, while giving competitors fits as they try to keep up. While not as driver-oriented as a BMW X5 or Mercedes-Benz GLE, the 2017 RX 350 and RX 450h hybrid provide more than acceptable levels of acceleration and handling, and still outshine almost every other luxury SUV in the areas of reliability and resale. The RX 350 isn't as roomy as a Lincoln MKX or Audi Q5, but it's loaded with advanced safety features optional on most other luxury brands. Those who don't mind sacrificing ride comfort for performance will find the F Sport trim quite alluring." -- Kelley Blue Book (2017)
