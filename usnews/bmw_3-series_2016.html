2016 BMW 3-Series Overview

Is the 2016 BMW 3 Series a Good Used Car?

Yes, the 2016 BMW 3 Series is a good used car. It is athletic and fun to drive and has spacious, comfortable seats. The 3 Series will appeal to a wide range of luxury car shoppers.

Used 2016 BMW 3 Series Performance and Interior

The BMW 3 Series has a range of powerful engines, from the turbocharged four-cylinder base engine to the twin-turbocharged six-cylinder powering the high-performance M3. There's also a fuel-efficient diesel engine. The base 3 Series gets great fuel economy of 23 mpg in the city and 35 mpg on the highway. The diesel is significantly better, earning 31 mpg in the city and 42 mpg on the highway. There's also an engine stop-start feature that helps conserve fuel, but it proves to be more annoying than useful. It can be disabled. The 3 Series has agile handling and a comfortable ride. Rear-wheel drive is standard and all-wheel drive is available.

Read more about 3 Series performance »

The BMW 3 Series' cabin is nice, but some rivals have more upscale interiors. The 3 Series seats five. The front seats are supportive, and the rear seats are among the most spacious in the class. There are two full sets of LATCH car-seat connectors.

Standard features include Bluetooth, a USB port, dual-zone automatic climate control, and the iDrive infotainment system with a 6.5-inch display. Notable options include a moonroof, navigation, satellite radio, and a 16-speaker Harman Kardon audio system.

Read more about 3 Series interior »

Used 2016 BMW 3 Series Prices

Used 2016 BMW 3 Series models begin around $32,900 for the base 320i trim and top out around $47,400 for the 340i xDrive. Keep in mind that the price of a used car varies depending on the trim you want, the vehicle's condition and mileage, and where you live.

How Reliable Is the 2016 BMW 3 Series?

The 2016 BMW 3 Series has a predicted reliability rating of 3.5 out of five from J.D. Power and Associates. This is a slightly above average score. 

Read more about 3 Series reliability »

How Safe Is the 3 Series?

The 2016 3 Series has a lower safety rating than many cars in the class, but it did alright in crash tests. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration gave the 3 Series five out of five stars overall, and it earned a rating of Good (the highest possible) in four tests performed by the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety.

The 3 Series doesn't have any notable standard active safety features. Several are available, including a rearview camera, adaptive cruise control, front and rear parking sensors, side- and top-view cameras, a head-up display, blind spot monitoring, and lane departure warning.

See 3 Series safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used 3 Series?

The 2016 BMW 3 Series is part of the vehicle's sixth generation, which began with the 2012 model year and includes the 2017 model. The 2016 model year is largely similar to the two previous years, so you can save yourself some money by going with a 2015 or 2014 model.

Compare the 2014, 2015, and 2016 3 Series »

Which Used BMW 3 Series Is Right for Me?

The 3 Series has several trim levels that are organized by engine. If you want a more powerful engine or an alternative powertrain – like the diesel engine – you'll have to move above the base model. When the 3 Series was new, there were also several options packages that you could add. Unless you want a specific engine, the best trim choices are the 328i and the 340i. They're the two most popular trims for a reason: they're not the most expensive, but they offer most of the driver assistance and tech features you'll want.

Like most automakers, BMW offers a certified pre-owned vehicle program. The BMW CPO program has three levels based on the vehicle’s age and the number of miles on the odometer: Certified Pre-Owned Elite, Certified Pre-Owned, and Pre-Owned. Each level receives the balance of the original new-car warranty. Certified Pre-Owned Elite vehicles get an additional one-year/25,000-mile warranty, while Certified Pre-Owned models receive an additional two-year/50,000-mile warranty. Pre-Owned BMWs receive no additional coverage. Each BMW undergoes an inspection before earning CPO status. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the BMW warranty page carefully.

2016 BMW 3 Series and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 BMW 3 Series or 2016 Audi A4?

The Audi A4 is a solid vehicle all-around. It has a strong engine that delivers good acceleration, as well as the high-performance S4 trim, which ups the ante over the standard A4 in terms of acceleration and power. Those looking for a sporty drive will appreciate the A4’s agile handling. Its ride is comfortable and its cabin is arguably the best in the class. Many reviewers agreed it surpasses the 3 Series' interior in terms of looks and luxury. The A4 has roomy front and rear seats, and the front seats offer enough support to keep you comfortable on long trips. The A4 also has above-average predicted reliability for the class. It's a close call, but the A4 is a slightly better car overall.

Which Is Better: 2016 BMW 3 Series or 2016 Cadillac ATS?

The Cadillac ATS has a lineup of engines ranging from a base four-cylinder to a twin-turbocharged V6, which puts out 464 horsepower in the performance-oriented ATS-V. The more powerful engines deliver forceful acceleration, ensuring that you have enough power for any situation. Inside, you'll find a cushioned ride and plenty of legroom in the front seats, though the rear seats are a bit cramped. There are plenty of tech features, including ample smartphone connectivity options and a Wi-Fi hot spot. There are numerous available driver assistance features to help you out when you're behind the wheel. Though there's plenty to like about the ATS, the 3 Series is still the better vehicle.
