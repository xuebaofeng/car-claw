2018 Jeep Wrangler Review

by Cody Trotter | July 19, 2018

Fully redesigned for 2018, the Jeep Wrangler sits close to the bottom of our compact SUV rankings. The Wrangler can tackle any terrain, but it's no daily driver, and its predicted reliability rating is poor.

Is the Jeep Wrangler a Good SUV?

In the compact SUV class, the Jeep Wrangler is one of the least practical options for everyday driving. However, it’s one of the best vehicles you can buy if you're looking for off-road prowess. Beyond its adventurous mentality, there are two solid powertrain choices for the Wrangler. The all-new Wrangler has a more modern interior with nicer materials and additional standard and available features. You'll also get more cargo space in the Wrangler than in many competitors.

However, the Wrangler's noticeable flaws stop it from being a clear-cut "good" SUV. It doesn't handle as well or ride as smoothly as many of its rivals, and it gets poor gas mileage estimates.

Should I Buy the Jeep Wrangler?

Consider purchasing the Wrangler if you love going off the beaten path. It's equipped with everything you need to stow your gear and hit the trails. You'll want to overlook the Wrangler if you don’t plan to tackle off-road trails often. Either way, the Toyota Tacoma, Toyota 4Runner, and Jeep Cherokee are good alternatives that are also worth a look.

Should I Buy a New or Used Jeep Wrangler?

The 2018 Jeep Wrangler is completely redesigned, so prior model years will be very different. You could opt for an older model to potentially save thousands of dollars, but you’ll miss out on the benefits of the redesign.

How Much Does the Jeep Wrangler Cost?

The Wrangler’s base price is $27,495. That’s a few thousand dollars more than most compact SUVs’ starting prices. Wrangler Unlimited models start at over $30,000, while the top trims start in the high $30,000s to low $40,000s. For that money, you could get a luxury compact SUV, so you’d better make sure the Wrangler’s off-road ability is worth its sky-high price.

Jeep Wrangler Versus the Competition

The Wrangler is unlike anything else on the market, which makes it a tough SUV to compare to others. This Jeep makes off-road ruggedness its first, second, and third priority. No other compact SUV can say that.

The redesigned 2018 Wrangler has improved interior technology and comfort that make it much more modern than its predecessors. That said, the Wrangler wasn't designed to provide the same level of convenience and comfort found in many other compact SUVs, so even the new model can't keep up with many class rivals' nicer cabins. In the end, some vehicles from other classes make the best comparisons.

Which Is Better: Jeep Wrangler or Jeep Cherokee?

If you're looking for a Jeep that is more comfortable than the Wrangler for everyday driving but still able to handle a good amount of off-roading, the Jeep Cherokee is a good choice. The Cherokee offers a trail-ready Trailhawk trim that's cheaper than many Wrangler Unlimited trims, and its suppler ride is better for driving around town.

The Cherokee’s seats provide all-day comfort and support, and there's enough space for adults. You can also slide the rear seats forward and backward, as well as recline them to make loading cargo easier and increase rear-passenger comfort. It comes with a good amount of technology features too. The Wrangler is a more capable off-road machine, but the Cherokee is better if you plan on mostly sticking to asphalt.

Which Is Better: Jeep Wrangler or Toyota Tacoma?

The Toyota Tacoma is a worthy alternative outside of the compact SUV class. This compact pickup truck's various off-road features and available four-wheel drive make it a highly capable off-road vehicle. For excellent performance off the pavement, look to the Tacoma TRD Off-Road and TRD Pro trims.

Moreover, the Tacoma can haul cargo better than the Wrangler. The Toyota can tow up to 6,800 pounds, and its maximum payload is over 1,600 pounds. The Tacoma’s well-built cabin comes with several standard safety features (some of which you won't find in the Wrangler), like adaptive cruise control, lane departure warning, automatic high beams, pedestrian detection, and forward collision warning. Choosing between the two will come down to your preferences. If you're looking for extra space and features in a capable off-roader, the Tacoma may be a better fit.

Which Is Better: Jeep Wrangler or Toyota 4Runner?

Consider purchasing the Toyota 4Runner over the Wrangler if you're looking for an SUV with similarly outstanding off-road capabilities but a bit more space and features. The 4Runner is a midsize SUV with a steeper starting price than the Wrangler; its base price is similar to higher Wrangler trims, like the Rubicon. However, you can still get the base 4Runner for less than many Wrangler Unlimited trims.

Additionally, the 4Runner's off-roading features – including a limited-slip differential and skid plates – contribute to its great off-road prowess. For more off-road-oriented features, consider the 4Runner TRD Pro trim. To increase seating capacity to seven, you can add a third row to the 4Runner. The 4Runner also delivers a significantly higher overall cargo capacity of nearly 90 cubic feet and a longer features list than the Wrangler.

Wrangler Performance

Wrangler Engine: New Model Year, New Engine Option

You can still get a Wrangler with a 285-horsepower V6 engine, as you’ve been able to do for years. In fact, that’s the base engine. But the redesign brings another option to the table: a turbocharged, 2.0-liter four-cylinder that produces 270 horsepower. This engine also has a mild electric power assist technology to improve fuel economy. The V6 is perfectly capable, but the turbo-four feels more energetic.

Wrangler Gas Mileage: It Isn’t Pretty

With the base engine, the Wrangler’s fuel economy ratings are abysmal for a compact SUV. It gets just 18 mpg in the city and 23 mpg on the highway. Numbers are slightly better with the turbo-four engine: 23 mpg in the city and 25 mpg on the highway.

It’s worth noting, however, that when compared against other 4WD-equipped vehicles with good off-road abilities, the Wrangler’s fuel economy doesn’t look as bad. The Toyota Tacoma with a V6 and four-wheel drive gets 18/22 mpg city/highway, and the Jeep Cherokee Trailhawk with a V6 and 4WD gets 18/24 mpg city/highway.

Wrangler Ride and Handling: Room for Improvement

The Wrangler’s firm suspension leads to a ride that’s stiff but not uncomfortable. You’ll feel bumps in the road, however. This Jeep is stable on winding roads, but it does lean in corners.

Wrangler Off-Road Performance

This Jeep is an off-road beast that few SUVs in any class can equal. Every trim comes with skid plates and part-time four-wheel drive, meaning you can drive the Wrangler out of a showroom and onto a trail. The higher trims have added features like sturdier suspensions and locking differentials that make them even more capable when the pavement ends.

Wrangler Towing Capacity

The Wrangler is rugged, but this compact SUV is still not great for towing. Two-door models can only tow up to 2,000 pounds, while four-door models can tow up to 3,500 pounds.

Wrangler Interior

How Many People Does the Wrangler Seat?

Two-door Wrangler models seat four people, and four-door models seat five. The front seats are a little short on legroom for tall people, but both rows have ample headroom. The Wrangler Unlimited has a large enough rear seat for adults, but the two-door Wrangler’s back seat is best left for kids.

Wrangler and Car Seats

The Wrangler features two full sets of LATCH connectors for installing car seats. They’re located in the rear outboard seating positions.

Wrangler Interior Quality

Upscale still isn’t a word you’ll throw around when describing the Wrangler, but the 2018 model is nicer than any Wranglers that have come before it. While it doesn’t match the upscale feel of some rivals, it does finally feel like a vehicle you can put your family in, rather than a bare-bones ride ready for a trip to the Thunderdome.

Wrangler Cargo Space

With the rear seats up, the Wrangler Unlimited has nearly 32 cubic feet of cargo space, which is plenty of room for a cooler and some beach chairs. Fold the rear seats down, and you’ll have more than 72 cubic feet of room – enough space to haul larger items like a bike or some skis.

The two-door Wrangler doesn’t have as much cargo room as the Unlimited. It offers just under 13 cubic feet of space behind the rear seats and under 47 cubic feet with the rear seats folded.

Wrangler Infotainment, Bluetooth, and Navigation

Standard features in the Wrangler include two USB and auxiliary ports, Bluetooth, an eight-speaker audio system, and the Uconnect infotainment system with a 5-inch touch screen. Available features include air conditioning, power windows, automatic climate control, an Alpine audio system, satellite radio, a Wi-Fi hot spot, and an upgraded Uconnect system with a 7- or 8.4-inch touch screen, navigation, Apple CarPlay, and Android Auto. With all these new standard and available tech features, Wrangler owners trading in an old model for a 2018 may have to take a minute to process what they’re seeing.

Wrangler Reliability

Is the Jeep Wrangler Reliable?

The 2018 Jeep Wrangler has a poor predicted reliability score for the class. J.D. Power gives it a below-average 2.5 out of five.

Jeep Wrangler Warranty

Jeep covers the Wrangler with a three-year/36,000-mile limited warranty and a five-year/60,000-mile powertrain warranty. Many other compact SUVs have similar warranty terms.

Read more about reliability »

Wrangler Safety

Wrangler Crash Test Results

The 2018 Jeep Wrangler has not yet been fully crashed tested, but it earns three out of five stars in the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration’s rollover and frontal crash tests.

Wrangler Safety Features

The Wrangler comes standard with a rearview camera. Available driver assistance features include blind spot monitoring, cross traffic alert, and rear parking sensors.

Which Jeep Wrangler Model Is Right for Me?

The Wrangler is offered in four trims, all of which come standard with a 285-horsepower V6 engine. It also comes in two body styles: the two-door base model and the four-door Wrangler Unlimited, which seats one extra person.

Choosing a Wrangler starts with choosing a body style. The four-door Wrangler Unlimited is the better choice if you plan to regularly seat passengers in the rear. If not, then you should stick with the two-door Wrangler and save yourself some money. The exception to this would be if you want the Sahara trim, which only comes as a four-door model.

Beyond body style, off-roading is a factor. The Sahara and Rubicon trims are more capable off-road, so you’ll want one of those if you plan to hit the trails every weekend. But for many buyers, the Wrangler Sport S is the best choice. It offers plenty of technology and still has the chops to handle serious off-roading, but it costs much less than the higher trims.

Jeep Wrangler Sport

The Wrangler Sport has a base price of $27,495 for two-door models and $30,995 for four-door models. It comes standard with USB and auxiliary ports, Bluetooth, an eight-speaker audio system, a rearview camera, and the Uconnect infotainment system with a 5-inch touch screen.

There aren’t a ton of optional features in the base trim, but you can upgrade from the standard six-speed manual transmission to an eight-speed automatic for $2,000. Opting for a hard top instead of the standard soft top will add another $1,095 to your price tag. Inside, you can add satellite radio for $295 and air conditioning for $1,295.

Jeep Wrangler Sport S

The Wrangler Sport S has a starting price of $30,695 for two-door models and $34,195 for four-door models. In addition to the base model’s features, the Sport S comes with air conditioning, power windows, and keyless entry.

The Sport S also has more available features than the base trim, including several option packages. The Cold Weather Group ($595) adds remote start, heated front seats, and a heated steering wheel. The Technology Group ($995) adds automatic climate control, satellite radio, and an upgraded Uconnect infotainment system with a 7-inch touch screen, Apple CarPlay, and Android Auto. The Jeep Active Safety Group ($895) adds blind spot monitoring, cross traffic alert, and rear parking sensors. You can also add an Alpine premium audio system for $1,295.

Jeep Wrangler Sahara

The Wrangler Sahara starts at $37,845. This trim is only offered with four doors. The Sahara comes with a heavy-duty suspension and specialized interior styling unique to this trim. It also comes with a 115-volt power outlet, automatic climate control, satellite radio, and an upgraded Uconnect system with a 7-inch touch screen, Apple CarPlay, and Android Auto.

In addition to some of the option packages listed above, the Sahara offers the Electronic Infotainment System Group ($1,495), which adds HD Radio, an Alpine audio system, and the highest-end Uconnect system with an 8.4-inch touch screen and navigation.

Jeep Wrangler Rubicon

The Wrangler Rubicon has a starting price of $37,495 for two-door models and $40,995 for four-door models. The Rubicon is the best off-roading trim. It comes with a performance suspension and a locking rear differential, but it otherwise has the same standard and available features as the Sahara.

The Final Call

The Jeep Wrangler is redesigned for the 2018 model year, and the improvements over the previous generation are immediately noticeable. It has a much more inviting interior and many more available features, but it retains the legendary Jeep Wrangler off-road ability. This isn’t the best choice for people who want an SUV as their daily driver, but if you’re looking for an SUV that can go anywhere, this Jeep should be near the top of your list.
