2016 Porsche Boxster Overview

Is the 2016 Porsche Boxster a Good Used Car?

The 2016 Porsche Boxster is an invigorating used luxury sports car. It offers an array of powertrain choices. Handling excels when the Boxster is thrown into curves, making it one of the most enjoyable two-seaters to drive. Ownership costs can run high; this is true of many luxury brands, but the Boxster is expensive to own even compared to its rivals.

Used 2016 Porsche Boxster Performance and Interior

The 2016 Porsche Boxster has a standard 265-horsepower 2.7-liter six-cylinder engine. Three more powerful six-cylinder motors are available: A 3.4-liter 315-horsepower power plant in the Boxster S, a 330-horsepower 3.4-liter motor in the Boxster GTS, and a top-of-the-line 3.8-liter 375-horsepower motor in the Boxster Spyder. All come standard with a six-speed manual transmission. A seven-speed automatic is available.

The base Boxster’s acceleration isn't sluggish, but it's not particularly fast. The Porsche Boxster S and GTS are better, and the Spyder, which shares an engine with the more expensive Porsche 911 Carrera S, is potent. Raw power isn’t the reason to buy a Boxster, though. The midengine, rear-wheel-drive Porsche Boxster tackles corners with enthusiasm, thanks to its responsive steering, strong grip, and powerful brakes. It rides firmly, but not uncomfortably.

If you’re among the minority that cares about fuel economy in a sports car, you’ll be pleased to know the 2016 Boxster gets an EPA-estimated 20 mpg in the city and 29 mpg on the highway with its base engine and manual transmission. The available automatic adds 2 mpg to those totals.

Read more about Boxster performance »

The 2016 Porsche Boxster seats two and comes with faux leather upholstery, manual height and fore/aft adjustment, and power-adjustable seatbacks. (The 2016 Porsche Cayman has seating for four if you need it, but it’s not a roadster.) Available features include leather upholstery, fully power-adjustable seats, a heated steering wheel, and heated and ventilated seats.

Standard features in the 2016 Porsche Boxster include a four-speaker audio system, Bluetooth, and an infotainment system with a 7-inch touch screen. A power-operated convertible fabric top is also standard. The Boxster's touch-screen infotainment system is easy to use, and the dashboard buttons are nicely laid out. In addition to the main touch screen, the Boxster has a display on its instrument cluster that shows useful information like navigation maps, tire pressure, and engine specs. The Boxster's top can be operated while moving at speeds of up to 31 mph. Some rivals require you to stop the car to operate the top.

The 2016 Porsche Boxster has two trunks. The front trunk has 5.3 cubic feet of cargo space, and the rear trunk is 4.6 cubic feet. That’s not a lot of space. However, lowering the top doesn’t take away any cargo room.

Used 2016 Porsche Boxster Prices

A used 2016 Boxster costs around $61,000 for the base trim, while the top-of-the-line Spyder is priced at about $96,000. Pricing can vary with vehicle condition, mileage, and location.

How Reliable Is the 2016 Porsche Boxster?

The 2016 Porsche Boxster has a predicted reliability rating of 4.5 out of five from J.D. Power and Associates. That’s considered well above average.

Read more about Boxster reliability »

How Safe Is the Boxster?

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS) did not crash test the 2016 Porsche Boxster. The Boxster has relatively few advanced safety features. It's available with a rearview camera, front and rear parking sensors, and an adaptive cruise control system, which can maintain a set following distance from the car ahead by automatically applying the gas and brakes.

See Boxster safety scores »

Should I Consider Another Used Boxster?

The 2016 Boxster is the fourth year of the Boxster’s third generation, which launched in 2012 and includes the 2017 model year. You might save money by considering a 2015 Porsche Boxster. It is largely the same as the 2016 Boxster, but it lacks a high-performance trim like the Spyder (which debuted for 2016).

Which Used Porsche Boxster Is Right for Me?

The 2016 Porsche Boxster comes in five trims: base, Black Edition, S, GTS, and Spyder. Standard features in the base model include a power-operated convertible fabric top, a 265-horsepower six-cylinder engine, faux leather upholstery, a four-speaker audio system, Bluetooth, and an infotainment system with a 7-inch touch screen. The Black Edition has the same engine as the base model, but is only available with black paint and includes dual-zone automatic climate control, larger wheels, front and rear parking sensors, heated seats, and an upgraded infotainment system with navigation and a seven-speaker stereo. The only difference between the Boxster S and the base Boxster is the S trim’s larger V6. The sportier GTS trim adds more power to the Boxster S engine, in addition to a throatier standard exhaust system and Porsche's adaptive suspension system.

The Boxster Spyder is the most hard-edged, performance-oriented trim. With a retuned version of the Porsche 911 Carrera S model’s six-cylinder engine, the Spyder is better suited for weekend track days than daily commutes. It also has a manual-folding top to save weight for better performance, and air conditioning and a stereo are standard.

Overall, the Boxster Black Edition is the best choice for comfortable daily commuting.

You may also want to consider a certified pre-owned Boxster. For its certified pre-owned vehicles, Porsche extends the original new-car warranty to six years or 100,000 miles. If the CPO vehicle is past its original new-car warranty term (four years/50,000 miles), Porsche will cover the CPO vehicle for two years or 50,000 miles – though Porsche places total mileage limits on this warranty. Each CPO Porsche must pass a 111-point inspection. Additional benefits like towing and roadside assistance may be available, so read the Porsche warranty page carefully.

2016 Porsche Boxster and Other Cars to Consider

Which Is Better: 2016 Porsche Boxster or 2016 BMW 2 Series?

The 2016 BMW 2 Series convertible has superb handling and very little body lean through tight turns. Its steering is responsive and provides good road feedback. You’ll also get two extra seats – albeit tiny ones. The 2 Series' interior isn't quite as luxurious as the 2016 Boxster's, but it still offers comfortable and supportive front seats. The 2 Series' M trim offers equal performance to the Boxster, but the Porsche has a higher predicted reliability rating, making it the better choice.

Which Is Better: 2016 Porsche Boxster or 2016 Chevrolet Corvette?

A used 2016 Chevrolet Corvette convertible should cost less than a Boxster S, and it delivers a lot more horsepower for the money – 140 horsepower, to be precise. The Corvette's potent standard V8 isn't the only trick this car has up its sleeve. It also has impressive cornering abilities, commendable braking power, and responsive steering. A comfortable ride makes the Corvette convertible a great weekend cruiser, and its 10-cubic-foot trunk makes it a more luggage-friendly choice than the Boxster. This American icon tops its German competition.
